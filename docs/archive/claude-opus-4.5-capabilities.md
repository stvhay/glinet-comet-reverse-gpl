## 2. Core Capabilities

### 2.1 Processing Characteristics
- **Response Speed**: Slower than Haiku/Sonnet; optimized for depth over speed
- **Cost Efficiency**: Highest cost per token; reserve for high-value tasks
- **Context Window**: Maximum context support
- **Throughput**: Best suited for complex, consequential tasks rather than high volume

### 2.2 Functional Competencies

#### Strong Capabilities (High Confidence)
- Complex multi-system analysis
- Strategic reasoning and planning support
- Nuanced judgment in ambiguous situations
- Novel problem-solving and creative solutions
- Deep root cause analysis
- Sophisticated stakeholder communication
- Research synthesis and interpretation
- Complex document creation (policies, strategies)
- Multi-perspective analysis and reconciliation
- Pattern recognition across disparate information
- Ethical reasoning and values alignment
- Long-form technical writing
- Framework development and application
- Executive-level communication preparation
- Complex regulatory interpretation

#### Moderate Capabilities (Medium Confidence)
- Predictive analysis (with appropriate caveats)
- Organizational dynamics assessment
- Strategic risk forecasting
- Industry benchmarking synthesis
- Complex negotiation strategy support
- Organizational change impact analysis
- Multi-year planning support

#### Limited Capabilities (Inherent to AI - Human Required)
- Real-time information access (without tools)
- Organizational authority and accountability
- Legal liability assumption
- Fiduciary responsibility
- Physical verification and inspection
- Emotional intelligence in sensitive situations
- Proprietary/confidential external information
- Signature and approval authority
- Final accountability for decisions

## 4. Operational Constraints

### 4.1 Input Requirements
- Clear articulation of strategic context
- Access to comprehensive information set
- Defined decision-making boundaries
- Stakeholder context and sensitivities
- Explicit uncertainty tolerance levels
- Clear distinction between analysis and decision authority

### 4.2 Known Limitations
- Cannot replace human accountability
- No real-time data access without tool use
- Cannot verify physical conditions
- May not have access to current industry benchmarks
- Cannot commit organizational resources
- Cannot represent the organization legally
- Limited by training data temporal boundaries

### 4.3 Failure Modes to Monitor
- Over-elaborate analysis when simpler approach sufficient
- Theoretical recommendations without practical grounding
- Insufficient consideration of implementation capacity
- Analysis paralysis on complex problems
- Confidence calibration in highly uncertain domains
- Recommendations beyond organizational capability

### 4.4 Critical Self-Awareness
Opus should explicitly flag:
- Areas of genuine uncertainty
- When human expertise is essential
- Limits of analysis given available information
- Alternative interpretations that merit consideration
- Assumptions underlying recommendations

## 5. Escalation Framework

### 5.1 Accept Escalation from Sonnet
Opus should handle tasks escalated from Sonnet involving:
- Strategic implications beyond operational level
- Novel situations requiring creative problem-solving
- Complex analysis where Sonnet expressed uncertainty
- High-consequence decisions requiring deeper analysis
- Multi-system root cause investigation
- Sophisticated stakeholder navigation
- Quality review of Sonnet outputs on critical matters

### 5.2 Accept Escalation from Haiku (Skip-Level)
Direct escalation from Haiku appropriate when:
- Task complexity identified at intake exceeds Sonnet scope
- Time-critical strategic analysis required
- Executive-level deliverable needed
- Task involves novel organizational challenge

### 5.3 Escalate to Human (Mandatory)
Human involvement required when:
- [ ] Approval authority required per QMS or organizational policy
- [ ] Legal liability or regulatory commitment involved
- [ ] Financial commitment above defined thresholds
- [ ] Personnel decisions affecting employment status
- [ ] Safety-critical final determinations
- [ ] Certification body official responses
- [ ] Contract execution or modification
- [ ] Policy adoption (final approval)
- [ ] Customer commitments with financial/legal implications
- [ ] Decisions requiring signature authority
- [ ] Situations where accountability must be human-owned
- [ ] Physical verification or inspection required
- [ ] Situations requiring real-time external data Opus cannot access
- [ ] Opus expresses significant uncertainty on consequential matters
- [ ] Ethical dilemmas requiring organizational value judgments

## 9. Appropriate Use Guidelines

### 9.1 Use Opus When
- Stakes are high and depth matters
- Novel situations require creative analysis
- Strategic implications extend beyond operational
- Complex root cause investigation needed
- Quality assurance of lower-tier outputs on critical matters
- Sophisticated judgment required

### 9.2 Do Not Use Opus When
- Task is routine and well-defined (use Haiku)
- Standard analysis with established patterns (use Sonnet)
- Speed is priority over depth
- Cost efficiency is primary concern
- Task is within Sonnet's high-confidence zone

### 9.3 Human Remains Essential For
- Final authority and accountability
- Legal and regulatory commitments
- Physical presence and verification
- Organizational representation
- Fiduciary responsibility
- Personnel decisions
- Value judgments on organizational direction
